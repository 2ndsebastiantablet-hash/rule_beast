# Rule Beast VR Art Direction

## Current Direction
A dark VR-inspired asymmetric horror arena with readable silhouettes, oppressive fog, and sharp crimson rule-symbols that signal monster power. The look is stylized rather than photorealistic: clean low-poly geometry, glossy wet floors, hard-edged light cones, and readable gameplay props.

## Style Sources
Haunted industrial maze, occult testing chamber, abandoned bunker, and tabletop horror board-game readability. Keep forms simple enough for first-person play while using lighting, fog, and scale to create tension.

## Palette And Lighting
Near-black blue floors and walls, cold cyan survivor/puzzle glows, sickly green interact lights, violet shadows, and crimson monster accents. Use sparse overhead lamps, emissive sigils, lantern cones, and dense dark fog.

## Characters And Props
Survivors are compact hazmat/VR-runner silhouettes with cyan visors. The monster is taller, angular, antlered or clawed, with red emissive eyes and rule-mark scars. Puzzle props are chunky ritual consoles with rotating symbols and clear progress lights.

## UI And Icons
HUD should feel like a cracked VR visor: translucent dark panels, cyan text for survivors, red warnings for monster pressure, round counter as a ritual rule list. Keep critical text large and high-contrast.

## Asset Generation Notes
Use generated 2D art sparingly for title/cards/icons if needed. Most world assets should be Three.js primitives with emissive materials and fog-friendly silhouettes.
